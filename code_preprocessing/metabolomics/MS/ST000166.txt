#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy Induces Adaptive Metabolic Transitions in the
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Profile of Heart Failure
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Background: Heart failure (HF) is associated with ventricular dyssynchrony and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	inefficiency, which can be alleviated by cardiac resynchronization therapy
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	The aim of this study was to determine the metabolomic signature in HF and its
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	value for the response to CRT. Methods: This prospective study consisted of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	patients undergoing CRT for advanced HF and 10 control patients who underwent
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	ablation for supraventricular arrhythmia but not CRT. Blood samples were
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	before and 3 months after CRT. Metabolomic profiling of plasma samples was
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	using gas chromatographymass spectrometry and nuclear magnetic resonance.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	The plasma metabolomic profile was altered in the HF patients, with a distinct
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	of metabolites, including Krebs cycle and lipid, amino acid, and nucleotide
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	CRT improved the metabolic profile. The succinate/glutamate ratio, an index of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	cycle activity, improved from 0.58?0.13 to 2.84?0.60 (P<.05). The
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	ratio, an indicator of the balance between glycolytic and fatty acid
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	increased from 0.96?0.05 to 1.54?0.09 (P<.01). Compared with the nonresponders
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	CRT, the responders had a distinct baseline plasma metabolomic profile,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	higher isoleucine, phenylalanine, leucine, glucose, and valine levels and lower
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	levels at baseline (P<.05). Conclusion: CRT improves plasma metabolomic
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	of HF patients indicating harmonization of myocardial energy substrate
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	CRT responders may have a favorable metabolic profile as a potential biomarker
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	predicting CRT outcome.
