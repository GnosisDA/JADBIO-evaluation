#STUDY
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	Analysis of the effects of Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors Sunitinib and Erlotinib on
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	Serum Metabolism In Vivo using Non-Targeted Metabolomics Analysis
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Background.More than 90 tyrosine kinases have been implicated in the
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	pathogenesis of malignant transformation and tumor angiogenesis. Tyrosine kinase
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	inhibitors (TKIs) have emerged as effective therapies in treating cancer by
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	exploiting this kinase dependency. The tyrosine kinase inhibitor erlotinib
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	targets epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR), whereas sunitinib targets
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	primarily vascular endothelial growth factor receptor (VEGRF) and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	platelet-derived growth factor receptor (PDGFR). TKIs impact the function of
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	non-malignant cells had have on- and off-target toxicities, including
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	cardiotoxicities. Most of the reports of sunitinib have identified cardiotoxic
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	effects, whereas erlotinib was less often found to have these effects. We
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	hypothesized that the deleterious effects of TKIs were related to their impact
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	on cardiac metabolism. Methods. C57BL/6 mice (10/group) were treated with
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	therapeutic doses of sunitinib (40 mg/kg), erlotinib (50 mg/kg), or vehicle
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	daily for 2 weeks. Echocardiographic assessment of the heart in vivo identified
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	significant systolic dysfunction consistent with cardiotoxicity compared to
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	vehicle treated controls. Heart, skeletal muscle, liver, and serum were flash
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	frozen and prepped for non-targeted GC-MS metabolomics analysis. Results.
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Compared to vehicle treated controls, sunitinib treated mice had significant
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	decreases insystolic function, whereas erlotinib treated mice did not.
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Non-Targeted metabolomics analysis of heart identified identified significant
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	decreases in Docosahexaenoic acid (DHA), Arachidonic Acid/Eicosapentaenoic acid
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	(EPA), O-Phosphocolamine, and 6-Hydroxynicotinic acid after sunitinib treatment.
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	DHA was significantly decreased in skeletal muscle (quadriceps femoris), with
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	elevations in cholesterol were identified in liver and elevated ethanol amine in
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	serum. In contrast, erlotinib affected only one metabolite elevated (spermidine
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	significantly increased).Conclusions.Mice treated with sunitinib had exhibited
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	systolic dysfunction within two weeks, with significantly lower heart and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	skeletal muscle levels of long chain omega-3 fatty acids docosahexaenoic acid
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	(DHA), arachidonic acid (AA)/eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA) and increased serum
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	O-Phosphocholine phospholipid. This is the first link between sunitinib-induced
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	cardiotoxicity and depletion in the polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	inflammatory mediators DHA and AA/EPA in the heart, possibly by affecting
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	mitochondria function where they have vital roles on calcium channels.
