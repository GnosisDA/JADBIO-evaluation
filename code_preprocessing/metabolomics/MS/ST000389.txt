#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Serum phosphatidylethanolamine levels distinguish benign from malignant solitary
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	pulmonary nodules and represent a potential diagnostic biomarker for lung
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	cancer.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Recent computed tomography (CT) screening trials showed that it is effective for
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	early detection of lung cancer, but were plagued by high false positive rates.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Additional blood biomarker tests designed to complement CT screening and reduce
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	false positive rates are highly desirable. In the current study, we expand upon
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	our initial experimental findings as part of the discovery phase by evaluating
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolites in serum from subjects with benign or malignant SPNs using a
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	combined approach of gas chromatography time-of-flight mass spectrometry
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	(GC-TOFMS) and hydrophilic liquid chromatography accurate mass quadrupole
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	time-of-flight mass spectrometry (HILIC-qTOFMS). Furthermore, we evaluated serum
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	collected pre-diagnosis and at-diagnosis of lung cancer in addition to samples
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	obtained post-surgical intervention from subjects with malignant SPNs
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	(post-diagnosis). We hypothesize that our systems biology approach to identify
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	candidate metabolomics biomarkers will ultimately lead to improved early
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	detection of lung cancer and can be used in as a companion blood test to LDCT
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	screening.

CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	diagnosis (>6 months prior to surgery, pre-diagnostic). Additional blood samples
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	were collected from some of the lung cancer patients, at-diagnosis (at
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	diagnosis) and post-treatment (after surgery). All but one patient had their
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	“at-diagnosis” sample collected before surgery, usually within days prior to
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	surgery, likely on their pre-operation visit. Surgery was usually performed
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	within 1–3 months of diagnosis. Diagnosis was made by biopsy and/or surgery
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	with all tissue diagnoses confirmed by pathology.
