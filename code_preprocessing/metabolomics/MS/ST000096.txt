#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	A study of changes in lipid metabolism of ovarian cancer cells co-cultured with
PR:PROJECT_TYPE                  	timecourse study
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	This West Coast Metabolomics Center pilot and feasibility project was granted
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Ernst Lengyel  (University of Chicago). The biology of ovarian cancer
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	is clearly distinct from that of most epithelial tumors, in that hematogenous
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	are rare, and ovarian tumors remain confined to the peritoneal cavity. The
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	a large pad of fat tissue (20x13x3cm) covering the bowel, is the most common
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	of OvCa metastasis. It consists primarily of adipocytes, which become the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	microenvironment for the OvCa cells. The underlying hypothesis for this
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	is that, in the presence of adipocytes, the metabolism of OvCa cells is
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	and shifts towards lipid utilization, which provides energy that facilitates
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	growth and metastasis. Preliminary results suggest that primary human omental
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	secrete cytokines which promote the metastasis of OvCa cells to the omentum and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	subsequent invasion. Once metastasis has occurred, OvCa cells induce lipolysis
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	omental adipocytes, and use the energy derived from these lipids to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	study the metabolic changes in the tumor microenvironment we have established a
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	organotypic culture of the human omentum using primary human cells established
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	patient tissue. Metabolic studies will be performed on adipocytes and OvCa
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	individually, on conditioned media and on adipocytes and OvCa cells co-cultured
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	our 3D model, with the goal of arriving at a comprehensive analysis of primary
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	and lipids in the tumor microenvironment. In the current investigation,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	analysis of primary metabolites and complex lipids were conducted on adipocytes
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	OvCa cells individually, on conditioned media and on adipocytes and OvCa cells
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	in our 3D model. Analysis of oxylipins was conducted on conditioned media. To
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	better understanding of the dynamic regulation of metabolic pathways we will
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	perform metabolic flux analysis using labeled cells (13C-glucose,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	in the 3D culture model. The primary objective of this study is to gain
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	into the dynamic interactions between OvCa cells and human adipocytes with the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	of elucidating targets of therapeutic intervention.
