#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Metabolomic-based investigation on the effects of antifungal agents in Candida
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Our aim is to determine the metabolic effects of increasing doses of an
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	agent on C. albicans metabolism (untargeted, steady state metabolomics). We
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	culture in vitro Candida cells to the mid-logarithmic growth in liquid media
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	at 37°C and then inoculate biological replicates (1ml) onto 22mm
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	filters under vocuum filtration in sterile conditions. Subsequently, isolates
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	be cultivated to midlogarithmic phase of growth on the same agar (RPMI-1640) to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	the antifungal agent has been added at a range of concentrations to achieve
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	equivalent to 0 MIC (no drug), 0.0625 MIC, 0.125 MIC, 0.25 MIC, 0.5 MIC and 1.0
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	at 37°C. At mid-logarithmic phase of growth (12h) replicates will be
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	quenched by immersion into a solvent mixture of 40% acetonitrile: 40% methanol:
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	water precooled at -40°C. The resulting quenched isolate/solvent mixtrue will
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	mechanically lysed by bead-beating with 0.1mm Zirconia beads in a tissue
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	and then centrifuged to seperate out cell wall components. Supernatants will be
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	and stored at -80°C until they will be sent to SECIM facility.
