#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Temporal metabolomic responses of cultured HepG2 liver cells to high fructose
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	and high glucose exposures
PR:PROJECT_TYPE                  	Time-course Study
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	High fructose consumption has been implicated with deleterious effects on human
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	health, including hyperlipidemia elicited through de novo lipogenesis. However,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	more global effects of fructose on cellular metabolism have not been elucidated.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	In order to explore the metabolic impact of fructose-containing nutrients, we
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	applied both GC-TOF and HILIC-QTOF mass spectrometry metabolomic strategies
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	using extracts from cultured HepG2 cells exposed to fructose, glucose, or
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fructose + glucose. Cellular responses were analyzed in a time-dependent manner,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	incubated in media containing 5.5 mM glucose + 5.0 mM fructose in comparison to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	controls incubated in media containing either 5.5 mM glucose or 10.5 mM glucose.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Mass spectrometry identified 156 unique known metabolites and a large number of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	unknown compounds, which revealed metabolite changes due to both utilization of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fructose and high-carbohydrate loads independent of hexose structure. Fructose
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	was shown to be partially converted to sorbitol, and generated higher levels of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fructose-1-phosphate as a precursor for glycolytic intermediates. Differentially
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	regulated ratios of 3-phosphoglycerate to serine pathway intermediates in high
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fructose media indicated a diversion of carbon backbones away from energy
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolism. Additionally, high fructoseconditions changed levels of complex
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	lipids toward phosphatidylethanolamines. Patterns of acylcarnitines in response
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to high hexose exposure (10.5 mM glucose or glucose/fructose combination)
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	suggested a reduction in mitochondrial beta-oxidation.

#COLLECTION
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	HepG2 cells (ATCC HB-8065) were cultured in MEM containing 10 % (v:v) FBS, 100
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	U/mL penicillin and 100 mg/mL streptomycin (Invitrogen, Carlsbad, CA), 1 x MEM
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	non-essential amino acids, and 5.5 mM glucose at 37 °C in a 5 % CO2
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	environment. Cells were grown for four to six passages in 10 cm tissue culture
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	dishes with 14 mL MEM, and transferred to MULTIWELL™ 12 well culture dishes
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	for incubation in 2 mL of the treatment medium. Upon reaching 80 % confluency,
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	the cell culture medium was changed to media representative of experimental
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	condition: 5.5 mM glucose MEM, 5.5 mM glucose MEM + 5 mM glucose, or 5.5 mM
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	glucose MEM + 5 mM fructose. Media was replenished after 24 and 48 h. Cell
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	material was collected at 10 min, 1, 6, and 24 h time points following the 48 h
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	media replacement. Culture dishes were placed on ice and each well was washed
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	twice with 1 mL ice-cold PBS. 4 mL ice-cold 3:1 methanol/H2O extraction solvent
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	was added to each well. Cell material was manually scraped from each well, and
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	the extraction solvent cell material suspension was transferred to collection
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	tubes and frozen at −80 °C prior to further processing.
