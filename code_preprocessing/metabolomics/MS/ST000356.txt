#SAMPLEPREP
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	A 50 µL aliquot of plasma/serum sample was spiked with two internal standard
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	solutions (10 µl p-chlorophenylalanine in water, 0.1 mg/mL; 10 µL
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	heptadecanoic acid in methanol, 1 mg/mL). The mixed solution was extracted with
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	175 µL of methanol: chloroform (3:1) and vortexed for 30 seconds. After storing
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	for 10 minutes at –20°C, the samples were centrifuged at 13,000 rpm for 10
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	minutes. An aliquot of 200 µL supernatant was transferred to a glass sampling
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	vial to vacuum dry at room temperature. The residue was derivatized using a
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	two-step procedure. First, 50 µL methoxyamine (15 mg/mL in pyridine) was added
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	to the vial and kept at 30°C for 90 minutes. After adding 10 µL C10-C40 (all
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	even alkanes, 12.5 µg/mL) as retention index, 50 µL N,O-bis-(trimetylsilyl)
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	trifluoroacetamide (BSTFA) (1% trimethylchlorosilane, TMCS) was added to the
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	samples, before being derivatized at 70°C for 60 minutes. Each 1 µL aliquot of
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	the derivatized solution was injected in splitless mode into an Agilent 6890N
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	gas chromatography coupled with a Pegasus HT time-of-flight mass spectrometry
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(Leco Co., St. Joseph, MI, USA). To minimize systematic analytical deviations,
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	each control sample was separated by 1 or 2 breast cancer samples. Breast cancer
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	samples from different stages were also run evenly in the whole experiment.
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	Separation was achieved on an Rxi-5 ms capillary column (Crossbond ® 5%
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	diphenyl/95% dimethyl polysiloxane, Restek, PA, USA), with helium as the carrier
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	gas at a constant flow rate of 1.0 mL/min. The temperatures of injection,
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	transfer interface, and ion source were set to 260, 260, and 210°C,
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	respectively. The GC temperature programming was set to 2 min isothermal heating
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	at 80°C, followed by 10°C/min oven temperature ramped to 220°C, 5°C/min to
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	240°C, and 25°C/min to 290°C, and a final eight minute maintenance at 290°C.
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	Electron impact ionization (70 eV) at full scan mode (m/z 40–600) was used,
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	with an acquisition rate of 20 spectra/second in the TOFMS setting. The data
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	generated in the GC-TOFMS instrument were analyzed by the ChromaTOF software
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(v4.33, Leco Co, CA, USA). Using the statistic component, the aligned comma
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	separated value (CSV) file can be obtained with sample information, peak
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	information and peak intensity. Peak areas of unique mass were normalized to the
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	internal standard. Compound identification was performed by comparing the mass
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	fragments with NIST 05 Standard mass spectral databases in NIST MS search 2.0
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	(NIST,Gaithersburg, MD, USA) software with a similarity of more than 70% and
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	reference standards (with retention time, or retention index if available in the
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	library, as another parameter). Internal standards and any known artificial
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	peaks, such as peaks caused by noise, column bleed and BSTFA derivatization
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	procedure, were removed from the dataset before statistical analysis.
