#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Unbiased profiling uncovers a crucial role for gut microbiome derived
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	metabolites in modulating GI epithelial cell damage and mitigating GVHD.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Taxonomic alterations in the intestinal microbiota are being progressively
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	associated with many diseases, including graft-versus host disease (GVHD).
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	However, the impact of these alterations on microbial metabolites and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	by-products and their subsequent impact on disease processes, such as GVHD, are
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	not known. Here we utilized a targetedn unbiased and blinded approach in a
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	blinded fashion to identify novel alterations in the levels of microbial
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolites, specifically levels including the short chain fatty acid (SCFA) and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	endogenous histone deacetylase inhibitor (HDACi), butyrate, after allo-BMT.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Surprisingly, alterations were observed only in intestinal epithelial cells
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	(IECs) but not in the luminal contents. The reduced butyrate in IECs (CD326+)
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	after allo-BMT resulted in decreased histone acetylation, which was restored
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	upon local administration of exogenous butyrate. This resulted in improved IEC
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	junctional integrity, increased anti-apoptotic proteins, decreased GVHD, and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	improved survival. Furthermore, alteration of endogenous microflora with 17
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	rationally selected strains of high butyrate producing Clostridia, also
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	decreased GVHD and increased survival following allo-BMT in experiments
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	performed at two different institutions. These data demonstrate an heretofore
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	unrecognized role of microbial metabolites and suggests that local and specific
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	alteration of microbial metabolites has direct salutary effects on GVHD target
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	tissues and mitigates its severity.

#TREATMENT
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	BMTs were performed as previously described {Reddy:2005dc}{Reddy:2008kl}.
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	Briefly, syngeneic (BALB/c ? BALC/b or C57BL/6 ? C57BL/6) and allogeneic
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	(C57BL/6 ? BALB/c or C3H.sw ? C57BL/6) recipients received lethal
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	irradiation. On day -1, BALB/c recipients received a total of 800 cGy of
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	irradiation (split dose separated by 3 hours) and B6 animals received a single
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	dose of 1000 cGy. Donor splenic CD90.2+ T cells were magnetically separated
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	using an autoMACs (Miltenyi Biotec; Bergisch Gladbach, Germany) and 0.5 x 106
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	– 1 x 106 T cells were transferred to BALB/c recipients and 2 x 106 T cells
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	were transferred to C57BL/6 recipients. 5 x 106 donor whole bone marrow was
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	transferred to all recipients.
#SAMPLEPREP
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	To determine targeted fatty acid quantitation, samples (plasma, spleen, liver,
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	intestine, and intestinal fecal content) from mice 7d and 21d post-transplant
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	were harvested, homogenized, and snap-frozen in liquid N2. Homogenized tissues
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	were normalized to their protein content, while 50µL of plasma was used per
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	sample and Equal volumes of plasma and homogenized tissue were utilized, while50
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	mg fecal content was weighed at necropsy before extraction. Samples were
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	dispersed in acidified water spiked with stable isotope-labeled SCFA standards
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	and extracted with diethyl ether.
