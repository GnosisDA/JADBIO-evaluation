#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Urine metabolomics to track disease progression in DMD
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) is an X-linked recessive form of muscular
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	dystrophy that affects males via a mutation in the gene for the muscle protein,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	dystrophin. Progression of the disease results in severe muscle loss, ultimately
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	leading to paralysis and death. Steroid therapy has been a commonly employed
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	method for reducing the severity of symptoms. This study aims to quantify the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	urine levels of amino acids and organic acids in patients with DMD both with and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	without steroid treatment. Track the progression of DMD in patients who have
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	provided multiple urine samples.

#COLLECTION
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	Subjects are consented and given a bag containing a urine collection cup,
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	antiseptic wipes and directions of how to give a clean catch urine sample. They
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	are then lead to a restroom where they collect their own urine sample using a
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	clean catch method (direction provided). Urine samples are then placed on ice
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	and brought to the UF CTSI Biorepository. There, samples are transferred into
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	500uL aliquots and stored at -80C. Samples will remain there until samples will
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	be removed and ran on the NMR for this study.
