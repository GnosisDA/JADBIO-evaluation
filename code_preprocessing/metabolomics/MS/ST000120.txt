PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	on the developing fetus
PR:PROJECT_TYPE                  	Pilot and Feasibility Projects
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Steroids play a broad and vital role in regulation of gene expression,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	sexual characteristics, maturation, reproduction, and neurological functions;
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	an imbalance in steroid metabolism is also linked to development and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	of many diseases including autism. Prenatal stress of different nature has been
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to affect both the mother and the offspring. Adverse nutritional conditions
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	gestation can impair the maternal hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis (HPA) and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	the fetus to high levels of glucocorticoids (GC). Evenwhen GC are required for
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	brain development; an increased exposure of the fetus to GC as a consequence of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	stress can affect fetal hypothalamic-pituitary-gonad axis (HPG) development,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	neurogenesis, and have a long term impact on the offsprings mental health.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	zinc availability can occur during pregnancy as a consequence of different
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	(nutritional deficiency, infections, diabetes, alcohol consumption, and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to certain toxicants). Importantly, several of these gestational conditions
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	been linked to autism. In fact, alterations in maternal zinc homeostasis upon
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to select environmental stressors (e.g. the phthalate plasticizer
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	phthalate (DEHP)) that have become increasingly common since the industrial
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	may underlie the recent rise in the incidence of autism.Alterations in maternal
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	homeostasis could expose the fetus to high GC concentrations secondary to a
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	maternal GC production and/or to a decreased capacity of the placenta to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	GC to inactive metabolites. The overall goal of this proposal is to investigate
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	alterations in zinc homeostasis during gestation triggered by either a marginal
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	nutrition or exposure to an environmental pollutant (the phthalate plasticizer
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	phthalate (DEHP)) can impair maternal and fetal endocrine signaling leading to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fetal brain development.
