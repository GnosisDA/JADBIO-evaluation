#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Metabolic profiling during ex vivo machine perfusion of the human liver
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	As donor organ shortages persist, functional machine perfusion is under
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	investigation to improve preservation of the donor liver. The transplantation of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	donation after circulatory death (DCD) livers is limited by poor outcomes, but
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	its application may be expanded by ex vivo repair and assessment of the organ
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	before transplantation. Here we employed subnormothermic (21 °C) machine
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	perfusion of discarded human livers combined with metabolomics to gain insight
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	into metabolic recovery during machine perfusion. Improvements in energetic
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	cofactors and redox shifts were observed, as well as reversal of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	ischemia-induced alterations in selected pathways, including lactate metabolism
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	and increased TCA cycle intermediates. We next evaluated whether DCD livers with
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	steatotic and severe ischemic injury could be discriminated from
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	‘transplantable’ DCD livers. Metabolomic profiling was able to cluster
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	livers with similar metabolic patterns based on the degree of injury. Moreover,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	perfusion parameters combined with differences in metabolic factors suggest
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	variable mechanisms that result in poor energy recovery in injured livers. We
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	conclude that machine perfusion combined with metabolomics has significant
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	potential as a clinical instrument for the assessment of preserved livers.

#SAMPLEPREP
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	1. Weigh 4 mg tissue sample ( muscle 20 mg) in to a 2.0 ml eppendorf tube. Add
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	1.0 mL extraction solvent to the tissue sample and using GenoGrinder homogenize
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	samples for 45 seconds ensuring that sample resembles a powder. 2. Centrifuge
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	the samples at 2500 rpm. for 5 minutes. Aliquot 2 X 500µl supernatant, one for
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	analysis and one for a backup sample. Store backup aliquot in the -20°C
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	freezer. 3. Evaporate one 500µl aliquot of the sample in the Labconco Centrivap
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	cold trap concentrator to complete dryness 4. The dried aliquot is then
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	re-suspended with 500l 50% acetonitrile (degassed as given) (only for liver
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	and brain samples). 5. Centrifuge for 2 min at 14000 rcf using the centrifuge
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	Eppendorf 5415. 6. Remove supernatant to a new Eppendorff tube. 7. Evaporate the
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	supernatant to dryness in the the Labconco Centrivap cold trap concentrator. 8.
SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	Submit to derivatization.
