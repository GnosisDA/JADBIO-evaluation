PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	The Malaria Host-Pathogen Interaction Center (MaHPIC)
PR:PROJECT_TYPE                  	Systems Biology
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	The Malaria Host-Pathogen Interaction Center (MaHPIC) is a transdisciplinary
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	malaria systems biology research program supported by an NIH/NIAID contract (#
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	HHSN272201200031C; see http://www.systemsbiology.emory.edu). The MaHPIC
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	generates many data types (e.g., clinical, parasitological, metabolomics,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	functional genomics, lipidomics, proteomics, immune response) and mathematical
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	models, to iteratively test and develop hypotheses related to the complex
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	host-parasite dynamics in the course of malaria in non-human primates, and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolomics data via collaborations with investigators conducting clinical
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	studies in malaria endemic countries, with the overarching goal of better
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	understanding human disease, pathogenesis, and immunity. Curation and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	maintenance of all data and metadata are the responsibility of the MaHPIC.

#STUDY
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	Experiment HuA: Metabolomics of plasma samples from humans infected
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	with Plasmodium vivax strain.
ST:STUDY_TYPE                    	Longitudinal study and treatment of multiple individuals with Chloroquine
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Patients with vivax malaria were enrolled in this study from June 2011 to
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	December 2012 at the Fundacão de Medicina Tropical Doutor Heitor Vieira Dourado
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	(FMT-HVD), an infectious disease referral center located in Manaus, Western
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Brazilian Amazon. This study, which required a 42-day follow-up period, was
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	approved by the FMT-HVD Institutional Review Board and the Brazilian National
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Ethics Committee (CONEP) (IRB approval #: CAAE: 12516713.8.0000.0005). All
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	protocols and documentation were reviewed and sample shipments approved by the
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Emory IRB. Male and female patients were eligible for inclusion if aged 6 months
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	to 60 years, bodyweight ?5 kg, presenting a blood parasite density from 250 to
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	100,000 parasites/microliter and axillary temperature ?37.5°C or history of
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	fever in the last 48 hours. Exclusion criteria were: use of antimalarials in the
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	previous 30 days, refusal to be followed up for 42 days and any clinical
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	complication. Patients received supervised treatment with 25 mg/kg of
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	chloroquine (CQ) phosphate over a 3-day period (10 mg/kg on day 0 and 7.5 mg/kg
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	on days 1 and 2). Primaquine (0.5 mg/kg per day for 7 days) was prescribed at
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	the end of the 42-day follow-up period. Patients who vomited the first dose
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	within 30 minutes after drug ingestion were re-treated with the same dose.
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Patients were evaluated on days 0, 1, 2, 3, 7, 14, 28 and 42 and, if they felt
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	ill, at any time during the study period. Blood smear readings, complete blood
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	counts, and diagnostic polymerase chain reaction (PCR) amplifications were
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	performed at all time points. Three aliquots of 100 µL of whole blood from the
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	day of a recurrence were spotted onto filter paper for later analysis by high
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) to estimate the levels of CQ and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	desethylchloroquine (DCQ) as previously described. In this study, CQ-resistance
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	with parasitological failure was defined as parasite recurrence in the presence
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	of plasma concentrations of CQ and DSQ higher than 100 ng/mL and microsatellite
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	analysis revealing the presence of the same clonal nature at diagnosis and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	recurrence. The CQ-sensitive control group consisted of patients with no
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	parasitemia recurring during follow-up period. A group of 20 healthy individuals
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	from Brazil was used as non-malarial control group. Within the MaHPIC, this
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	project is known as ‘Experiment HuA’. This dataset was produced by Dean
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Jones at Emory University.
