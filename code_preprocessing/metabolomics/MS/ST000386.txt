PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Study1 Investigation of metabolomic blood biomarkers for detection of
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	adenocarcinoma lung cancer
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Recently, the National Lung Cancer Screen Trial (NLST) demonstrated that
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	low-dose CT (LDCT) screening could reduce mortality due to lung cancer by 20%.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	However, LDCT screening is largely hindered by high false-positive rates (96%),
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	particularly in high-risk populations (heavy smokers), due to the low prevalence
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	rates (less than 2%) of malignant tumors and high incidence of benign lung
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	nodules. Consequently, complementary biomarkers that can be used in conjunction
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	with LDCT screening to improve diagnostic capacities and reduce false-positive
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	rates are highly desirable. Preferably, such complementary tools should be
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	noninvasive and exhibit high sensitivity and specificity. The application of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	“-omic” sciences (genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics)
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	represents valuable tools for the discovery and validation of potential
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	biomarkers that can be used for detection of NSCLC. Of these omic sciences,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolomics has received considerable attention for its application in cancer.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Metabolomics is the assessment of small molecules and biochemical intermediates
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	(metabolites) using analytic instrumentation. Metabolites in blood are the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	product of all cellular processes, which are highly responsive to conditions of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	disease and environment, and represent the final output products of all organs
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	forming a detailed systemic representation of an individual's current
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	physiologic state. In this study, we used an untargeted metabolomics approach
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	using gas chromatography time-of-flight mass spectrometry (GCTOFMS) to analyze
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	the metabolome of serum and plasma samples both collected from the same patients
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	that were organized into two independent case–control studies (ADC1 and ADC2).
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	In both studies, only NSCLC adenocarcinoma was investigated. The overall
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	objectives were to (i) determine whether individual or combinations of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolites could be used as a diagnostic test to distinguish NSCLC
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	adenocarcinoma from controls and (ii) to determine which, plasma or serum,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	provides more accurate classifiers for the detection of lung cancer. We
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	developed individual and multimetabolite classifiers using a training test from
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	the ADC1 study and evaluated the performance of the constructed classifiers,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	individually or in combination, in an independent test/validation study (ADC2).
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	This study shows the potential of metabolite-based diagnostic tests for
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	detection of lung adenocarcinoma. Further validation in a larger pool of samples
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	is warranted.

TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	Subjects were recruited over a 4-year period (2010–2014) from the UC Davis
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	Medical Center and Cancer Center Clinics. All subjects were diagnosed with NSCLC
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	adenocarcinoma before specimen collection. The control population was heavily
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	recruited from spouses and family members accompanying a lung cancer patient to
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	their clinic to maintain as much of a similar environment and life styles,
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	especially diet and smoking history, as possible. Cases were frequency matched
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	with controls for gender, age, and smoking history. Only cases diagnosed with
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	NSCLC adenocarcinoma were used in these studies. Fasting status was not
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	controlled for as individuals were recruited upon their arrival to the clinic.

TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	plasma samples obtained from 52 stages I–IV NSCLC adenocarcinoma patients (52

TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	discovery and classifier development. A second, independent case–control study
